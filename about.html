About

A research program with a practical purpose.

Uni Student Wellbeing is a multi-disciplinary research program contributing to young adults’ mental health and wellbeing in Australia’s universities. Based at the University of Melbourne, it combines rigorous research with free, actionable resources for educators.

What we do

Three programs, one purpose

The USW program operates across three interconnected streams: original research into the conditions that shape student mental health, a Research Hub that disseminates findings and builds community, and a set of free learning modules that give educators practical tools grounded in that research.

The goal is not just to understand student wellbeing but to build the capacity of academic educators to design curriculum and create teaching and learning environments that actively promote it.

Our research approach

How we work

01

Multi-method

We combine large-scale longitudinal surveys with qualitative interviews and policy analysis. Different methods illuminate different aspects of the same underlying questions about student wellbeing.

02

Multi-institutional

The project runs across five Australian universities, with international collaboration through partners at UCLA and King’s College London. Our findings reflect the diversity of the higher education sector.

03

Multi-stakeholder

We work with student representatives, academic staff, professional staff, and university leadership. Wellbeing isn’t one group’s responsibility, so the research isn’t one group’s perspective.
