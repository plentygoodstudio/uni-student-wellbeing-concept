FAQ

Common questions

Can I use the learning modules in my own teaching?
Yes. All modules are licensed under Creative Commons BY-SA 4.0, which means you can use, adapt, and share them with attribution. Many universities embed the modules in their professional development programs.
Are the modules accredited or certified?
The modules don’t currently offer formal accreditation. Some institutions issue their own certificates of completion when staff finish a module. Contact your faculty’s learning and teaching unit to ask.
How can I get involved in the research?
We’re always interested in connecting with researchers and educators working on student wellbeing. For collaboration enquiries, contact Professor Chi Baik directly. For media or media partnerships, use the general enquiry form.
Is the research data publicly available?
De-identified data from the USW Survey is available to researchers on request, subject to our data sharing agreement. Contact the research team for details.
Can my university partner with the project?
Yes. We currently work with five Australian universities and welcome additional partners. Reach out to discuss what partnership might look like for your institution.
Do you offer training or workshops?
We run occasional workshops and webinars through the Research Hub. Subscribe to updates to hear when new sessions are announced.
