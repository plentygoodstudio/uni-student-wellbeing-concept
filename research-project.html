USW Research Project

How do university environments shape student mental health?

An ARC Discovery Project running from 2023 to 2026 across five Australian universities. We're investigating the relationship between higher education conditions and student psychological wellbeing, and what institutions are positioned to change.

What we're investigating

Four research questions

The project is structured around four interconnected questions about students, educators, and higher education institutions.

1

How do the conditions of higher education (learning environments, assessment design, workload, and belonging) affect student psychological wellbeing?

2

What role do curriculum design and teaching practice play in supporting or undermining student mental health across different disciplines?

3

How do students' sense of belonging and perceived value of higher education relate to their wellbeing outcomes over time?

4

What institutional and individual factors enable educators to implement wellbeing-informed curriculum and teaching practice at scale?

A rigorous, multi-method program.

The USW Research Project employs quantitative and qualitative methods across three concurrent studies, spanning five Australian universities.

Active studies

Three research streams

Uni Student Wellbeing Survey

A large-scale annual survey tracking psychological wellbeing, academic engagement, and support-seeking behaviours among students at five Australian universities. Now in its third year.

Learn more about the survey →

Students' Sense of Belonging Study

Longitudinal qualitative and quantitative study examining how belonging (to their institution, discipline, and peer group) shapes student wellbeing trajectories across a degree.

Learn more about the belonging study →

Value of Higher Education Study

Investigating how students' perceived value of their education (personally, socially, and economically) relates to their mental health and academic outcomes.

Learn more about the value study →
